# Bank Statement Red Flags

## Signal hierarchy

Analysts should separate isolated operational noise from repeated deterioration:

- A single NSF fee can be structured around when DSCR remains above 1.25x and liquidity is otherwise healthy.
- Two or more NSF or returned payment events within 90 days are a strong manual-review signal.
- Five or more negative balance days usually indicate a decline unless a verified timing explanation is documented.

## Liquidity stress patterns

The following statement patterns are more concerning than headline revenue alone:

- Marketing, inventory, or contractor debits posting materially ahead of collections.
- Tax or payroll debits that are delayed, fragmented, or replaced with partial payments.
- Frequent same-day sweeps that leave the operating account near zero before customer cash lands.

## How analysts should respond

Use structure when the business is otherwise healthy:

- Add a reserve when the file has one isolated NSF event or a shallow minimum balance dip.
- Shorten reporting cadence when balances repeatedly approach zero but do not turn negative.
- Escalate to manual review when statement stress appears alongside customer concentration or rising chargebacks.
