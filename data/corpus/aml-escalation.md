# AML Escalation and Enhanced Due Diligence Playbook

## Escalation objective

AML operations exists to distinguish normal growth from suspicious pattern changes without turning every unusual merchant into a full investigation. The escalation policy relies on risk thresholds so the team can move quickly on truly concerning behavior and avoid drowning analysts in noise.

## When enhanced due diligence is required

Enhanced due diligence is required when beneficial ownership is opaque, transaction flows touch high-risk corridors, volume accelerates sharply after onboarding, or counterparties appear sanctions-adjacent even if the names are not exact matches. EDD is also required when the merchant changes business model faster than the original KYC file can explain. The goal is to verify that the original onboarding story still matches the live transaction pattern.

## KYC refresh triggers

KYC refresh is triggered by material ownership changes, missing source-of-funds evidence, document expiry, or new behavior that pushes the merchant into a different risk tier. A simple annual refresh cadence is not enough for fast-moving fintech portfolios because the highest-risk merchants tend to drift between reviews.

## Filing and governance

When AML escalates a case, the analyst records the triggering signal, the missing evidence, and the reason ordinary monitoring is no longer sufficient. If the investigation still cannot reconcile the flow pattern, the case moves toward suspicious activity review. Governance matters here because unsupported escalation is expensive, but delayed escalation creates regulatory and reputational risk.

## Operating principle

The playbook prefers documented thresholds over intuition. Analysts can still use judgment, but the judgment is expected to explain why the merchant deviated from the known low-risk pattern in a way that ordinary monitoring would miss.
