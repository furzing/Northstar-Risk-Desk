# Revenue Quality Framework

## Recurring revenue

The cleanest cash flow borrowers usually show repeat invoicing or repeat processor payouts from the same customer set:

- Recurring revenue above 60% supports higher confidence in forward collections.
- Recurring revenue below 40% requires more caution because the next quarter may not resemble the last quarter.

## Concentration

Concentration drives both repayment risk and structure:

- Top customer concentration below 30% is strong.
- Concentration between 30% and 40% is acceptable with monitoring.
- Concentration above 40% should usually trigger reserve, tighter covenants, or a lower advance rate.

## Volatility and leakage

Revenue quality weakens when the gross sales line is offset by leakage:

- Monthly inflow volatility above 25% should reduce confidence in straight-line forecasting.
- Refunds and chargebacks above 1% of gross sales should move the file into heightened review.
- Fast growth is not enough on its own if cash conversion is getting worse.
