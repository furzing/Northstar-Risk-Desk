# Fraud And KYB Overrides

## When fraud signals intersect with underwriting

Some files look financeable on gross cash flow but require an underwriting override because the operating pattern points to fraud or control gaps:

- Processor concentration plus rising chargebacks can signal channel abuse or poor fulfillment.
- Sudden refund spikes without matching revenue growth can mean hidden product issues.
- Ownership or bank-account mismatches discovered during KYB should block approval until resolved.

## Practical treatment

For borderline files, combine fraud signals with credit signals instead of treating them separately:

- One weak signal may justify only a reserve or shorter tenor.
- Several weak signals together should push the file into manual review even when DSCR is technically above 1.00x.

## Operating note

E-commerce borrowers deserve tighter interpretation because ad spend can temporarily mask weak unit economics. In that segment, rising chargebacks and declining liquidity should be treated as a linked problem rather than separate isolated events.
