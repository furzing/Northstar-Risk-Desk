# Invoice Advance Policy

## Eligible receivables

Receivables can support an invoice advance only when the work is complete and the paper is collectible:

- Invoice due dates should be 60 days or less from issue.
- The obligor must be identifiable and creditworthy.
- No known offsets, disputes, or contra balances should exist.
- Overdue invoices are not eligible for the base borrowing formula.

## Advance-rate bands

Advance rates should tighten as obligor risk rises:

- 80% to 85% advance rates are reserved for low-dilution books with overdue receivables below 5% and obligor concentration below 30%.
- 70% to 75% advance rates fit concentrated but still collectible books where top customer concentration is between 30% and 45%.
- Advance rates below 70% or outright ineligibility are appropriate when aging exceeds 15%, dilution is elevated, or documentation is incomplete.

## Ongoing monitoring

Invoice lines require more than an origination decision:

- Recalculate eligibility weekly when the borrower is growing quickly.
- Freeze new advances when aging breaches 18% or when the largest obligor misses a payment date.
- Step down the advance rate before moving to a full block whenever the deterioration is still reversible.
